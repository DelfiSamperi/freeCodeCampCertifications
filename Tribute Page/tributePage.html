Cecilia Grierson

The first woman to receive a Medical Degree in Argentina

A time line of Cecilia's life:

  • 1859 - Born in Buenos Aires on November 22, 1859, the daughter of Scottish immigrants.
  • 1878 - Completed her studies as a primary school teacher at Girls Normal School of Buenos Aires, although she had been teaching without a formal qualification since the age of 14 (something common due to the lack of teachers in rural areas).
  • 1883 - Entered the Faculty of Medical Sciences, where she graduated six years later, in 1889. During these six years, she became an assistant in the Histology Laboratory.
  • 1889 - Published her thesis "Hystero-ovariotomies performed at the Women’s Hospital from 1883 to 1886" for her Medical degree.
  • 1889 - As soon as she graduated, she joined Hospital San Roque, where she dedicated herself to gynecology and obstetrics, but not surgery, a specialty she was denied due to her gender, despite having the qualifications to practice it.
  • 1891 - Founding member of Argentinian Medical Association, where she was elected president on 1907.
  • 1892 - Founded the Argentinian Society of First Aid, which later merged with the Argentinian Red Cross. She also pioneered the idea of opening First Aid Rooms in various towns to assist with primary care for the sick.
  • 1892 - Collaborated with the team led by Samuel Molina in performing the first cesarean section in Argentina at what was then the Women’s Hospital, now Hospital Rivadavia.
  • 1901 - Founder of the National Obstetrics Association and the Obstetrics Journal, which she conceived as a tool to offer Argentinian midwives a scientific and medical approach to the profession.
  • 1904 - 1905 - Taught the first courses on "Medical Gymnastics and Kinesiotherapy" at the Faculty of Medicine, marking the first steps in the development of kinesiology in Argentina. Additionally, she was an adjunct professor in the Medical Physics and Obstetrics Chair.
  • 1913 - Her last year as Director of the first School of Nursing in Latin America, which she founded with a formal curriculum.
  • 1935 - Passed away on April 10, at the age of 74.

Feminist Activism

  • 1900 - Established the Argentinian Women’s Council (CNM) after being named Vice President of the second meeting of the suffragist organization, the International Council of Women (ICW), held in London in 1889.
  • 1904 - Co-founded the Association of Argentine University Women (AMUA), the first university student association for women in the country, with other thirty women from CNM.
  • 1910 - Chaired the First International Feminist Conference of Argentina organized by the Association of University Women during the Argentinian Centennial. This provoked her departure from the CNM.
